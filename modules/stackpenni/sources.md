# Source Criteria — v2.0

## Summary
StackPenni's trusted sources suggest a strong preference for content that bridges deep philosophical/psychological foundations with practical wealth-building and regional Caribbean development. The operator values a mix of timeless texts (philosophy, religion, psychology), academic papers on financial behavior (like Klontz Money Scripts), and highly specific regional reports on Caribbean culture (Landships, ROSCAs) and financial literacy (ECCU, Central Bank of Barbados). Good sources are either authoritative global insights that transfer to the Caribbean context or original data/cultural analyses directly addressing the region. Formats range from books and PDFs to impactful social media quotes, provided they offer substantive, non-generic insights into AI, wealth, and human behavior.

## Freshness
Timeless is fine if foundational (philosophy, psychology, classic literature), but recent reports and articles (within 1-2 years) are required for financial literacy data, AI trends, and regional developments.

## Quality signals
- **Authoritative Authors and Thinkers** — Sources written by or featuring recognized intellectuals, entrepreneurs, or psychologists. (evidence: Thomas Sowell — Intellectuals, Social Justice, and the Urge to Power, The Algebra of Wealth, @profgalloway..., About speaker: Steven Paul Jobs...)
- **Regional Relevance and Data** — Sources that specifically address Caribbean economics, culture, or financial systems with original data or deep cultural context. (evidence: ECCU Financial Literacy and Financial Inclusion Survey Report (2022/2023), Credit, Identity, and Resilience in the Bahamas and Barbados, Building Economic Solidarity: Caribbean ROSCAs in Jamaica, Guyana, and Haiti)
- **Behavioral and Psychological Insights** — Sources that explore the psychology behind money, success, and human behavior. (evidence: Money Beliefs and Financial Behaviors: Development of the Klontz Money Script, The Cultural Origin of Saving Behavior, Joe Rogan on Breaking Free from Quiet Desperation)
- **Practitioner Experience** — Sources detailing real-world entrepreneurial strategies and personal financial journeys. (evidence: Airbnb Automation: My 1 to 150 Property Arbitrage Strategy (Passive Income System), Got my Mortgage at 23, now I’m over 40 Im embarrassed 🙈 to admit how green 💚 I was.)

## Disqualifiers
- **Content-mill SEO and superficial regurgitation** — evidence: Inferred from lack of low-quality sources in the seed list; operator favors deep, original texts and reports.
- **Clickbait titles with no substantive insight** — evidence: Inferred from the preference for academic, philosophical, and data-driven sources.
- **Generic financial advice lacking cultural or psychological context** — evidence: Inferred from the specific focus on Klontz Money Scripts and Caribbean ROSCAs rather than generic budgeting tips.

## Regional relevance
Global sources are highly valued if the insights transfer to wealth-building, psychology, or entrepreneurship. However, sources specifically addressing Caribbean culture, regional financial systems (ROSCAs, ECCU), and the impact of AI/digital transformation on the Caribbean are essential and uniquely favored.
