# Visual Style Guide — v1.0

## Summary
StackPenni's visual identity blends Caribbean warmth with fintech credibility. The palette draws from deep ocean teal, Caribbean coral, and prosperity gold on a warm sand background — culturally rooted but financially serious. Typography should feel confident and approachable, with bold headlines and readable body text built for mobile. Stylization sits at moderate: real footage anchors all trust-critical claims (people, places, products, results), while generated visuals support abstract concepts, transitions, and cultural texture. X leans text-forward with real footage and clean data cards; Instagram leans visual-first with more room for Caribbean color and motion. The shot library is the primary visual source for every draft — generated visuals fill conceptual gaps only, never replace real footage, and are disclosed per platform rules when prominent.

## Palette
- **Primary:** Deep Ocean Teal (#0A4D5C)
- **Secondary:** Caribbean Coral (#E8654A)
- **Accent:** Prosperity Gold (#F2B705)
- **Background:** Warm Sand Cream (#FAF6F0)

## Typography
- **Feel:** Confident and modern with Caribbean warmth — clean fintech clarity meets approachable cultural energy. Not corporate-stiff, not decorative. The type should feel like a trusted advisor who also knows how to celebrate.
- **Weight:** Bold for headlines and key numbers; regular for body. Light weight reserved for large display only. Numbers and financial figures should carry visual weight — they are the proof.
- **Sizing:** Strong hierarchy with generous scale jumps between headline, subhead, and body. Headlines dominate; body text stays highly readable on mobile. Captions and overlays sized for thumb-distance reading, not desktop.

## Stylization level
moderate

StackPenni sits at the intersection of Caribbean culture (vibrant, expressive, warm) and financial/tech literacy (trustworthy, precise, credible). Heavy stylization risks undermining financial credibility; minimal stylization undersells the cultural identity. Moderate allows generated visuals to carry cultural texture and conceptual metaphors while real footage anchors the trust-critical claims. This is a starting hypothesis — the inward loop will refine based on audience response.

## Blend rules

### Real anchors (require real footage)
- Real people — Caribbean entrepreneurs, community members, founders in their actual environments
- Real locations — Caribbean streets, markets, workspaces, neighborhoods that ground cultural claims
- Real screens and products — actual app interfaces, dashboards, financial tools being used
- Real events and moments — workshops, meetups, hands-on activity that proves StackPenni shows up in the real world
- Any claim about specific people, places, results, or partnerships

### Generated supporting (what AI visuals are for)
- Abstract financial concepts — wealth building, compounding, AI as a tool, digital transformation as a process
- Transitions and motion backgrounds that carry brand color and texture without depicting real subjects
- Data visualizations and infographic-style illustrations that simplify complex ideas
- Cultural motif overlays and decorative elements that enhance but do not replace real Caribbean footage
- Conceptual metaphors — growth, momentum, connection — rendered as stylized supporting layers

### Disclosure (platform AI-disclosure rules)
- X: Label AI-generated visuals in the post text or alt text when they are the primary visual; real footage with generated overlays does not require disclosure if real footage is dominant
- Instagram: Use platform AI-disclosure labels for fully generated images; for hybrid real+generated content, add a small on-screen marker or caption note when generated elements are prominent
- Never present a generated visual as a real person, real location, or real event
- When in doubt, disclose — trust is the product

## Platform adjustments
- **X (@StackPenni)** — 16:9 for primary visuals; 1:1 acceptable for quotes and data cards: Text-forward platform — visuals should support the written take, not replace it. Headline overlays in Deep Ocean Teal with Prosperity Gold highlights. Keep generated visuals subtle; real footage or clean data cards perform better here. Alt text required on all images.
- **Instagram (@stackpenni)** — 1:1 for feed posts; 4:5 for portrait feed; 9:16 for Stories and Reels: Visual-first — Caribbean color and texture can breathe more here. Reels may use generated visuals, real footage, or a mix — the pipeline handles visual generation from the script. Prefer real footage when available, but generated visuals are a valid primary for Reels. Carousel posts can mix real photos with generated concept slides. Captions styled with line breaks and emoji used sparingly for cultural warmth, not clutter.

## Shot library usage
The indexed shot library is the primary visual source for all drafts. When a draft references a real person, location, product, or event, pull from the library first — never substitute generated visuals for something the library already contains. Generated visuals are used only when the library lacks coverage for an abstract concept or transition. Each draft should cite which library shots it uses by index so the operator can verify real footage is anchoring trust. If the library is thin in a needed area, flag it for the operator rather than filling the gap with generation.
