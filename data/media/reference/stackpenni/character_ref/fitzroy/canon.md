## Identity

- **Full name:** Fitzroy Pennifold
- **Nickname:** "Shillings" — his generation's money, as "Stacks" is his grandson's. Barbados traded in shillings and pence until 1973; the two nicknames carry the island's currency history across the family.
- **Age:** 74 (fixed — does not age between episodes)
- **Relation:** grandfather of Stackwell "Stacks" Pennifold
- **Brand role:** the story engine. Lead of the first-person parable episodes — confession-style, past tense, one hard-won lesson per episode. Regret and hindsight are only credible from a man with a past; that man is Fitzroy.
- **Narrative role:** the teller. In two-hander scenes he answers what Stackwell asks. He teaches through his own failures and recoveries, never through abstraction.

## Face canon (verbatim prompt block)

> Elderly Black Barbadian man, age 74, deep brown weathered skin, close-cropped white-grey hair, neat short white-grey beard, deep smile lines and lined forehead, kind heavy-lidded eyes, thin gold-rimmed reading glasses often pushed up or held in hand, medium build with a straight proud posture.

## Wardrobe canon (verbatim prompt block)

> Cream short-sleeve guayabera shirt with subtle vertical pleats, dark navy trousers, worn gold ring on right hand, brown felt trilby hat worn or resting nearby. No other jewelry, no modern athletic wear.

Wardrobe is fixed. The trilby may be on-head or on-table depending on setting (indoors: on the table).

## Signature props

- Decades-old black notebook, corners softened, elastic band — the original that Stackwell's *Plan Save Invest Repeat* journal descends from
- Wooden walking stick with a small brass broken-trident head
- Enamel cup of tea or mauby (never branded drinks)
- The reading glasses — his thinking gesture is removing them

## Voice register (VO and captions)

Past tense, measured, unhurried Bajan cadence. Speaks in specifics — years, amounts, what things cost then — and closes each parable by naming the lesson plainly in one sentence. Proverbs used naturally, at most one per episode. Never bitter, never nostalgic-for-nostalgia's-sake: he is a man who figured it out late and wants the boy to figure it out early.

## Settings he appears in

Primary: the gallery porch (his home turf and the two-hander stage), the kitchen table, the village rum shop exterior (he drinks tea there — deliberate), the market in town, the beach at dusk for closing shots. See WORLD-pennifold-canon for locked location references.
